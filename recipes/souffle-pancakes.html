Souffle Style Pancakes

Description

These souffle style pancakes are made by folding an egg white meringue into the batter. The batter is then piped into tall mounds in a pan and steamed. Served with a quick and easy blueberry sauce.

Ingredients

Pancakes
  • 1/4 cup white sugar, divided
  • 2 large eggs, separated
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • whipped cream, for serving
Blueberry Sauce
  • 1 cup frozen blueberries
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar

Steps

  1. For blueberry sauce: combine blueberries, water, lemon juice, and sugar in a small pot. Cook over low-medium heat, stirring occasionally, until thickened, 5 to 7 minutes. Remove from heat.
  2. For pancakes, whisk 2 tablespoons sugar, egg yolks, milk, vanilla, and a pinch of salt together in a bowl. Sift flour and baking powder over yolk mixture. Whisk until smooth.
  3. Whip egg whites with cream of tartar until frothy, then slowly stream in remaining 2 tablespoons sugar while mixing. Continue whipping until stiff, glossy peaks form.
  4. Fold half of meringue into the pancake mixture until 75% is incorporated (tip: fold lightly to keep it airy). Fold in remaining meringue, stirring just until the last streaks disappear.
  5. Heat a nonstick skillet over low heat. Grease with a neutral oil and wipe with a paper towel to leave only a thin layer.
  6. Add batter to a piping bag or resealable plastic bag. Snip a large hole in the corner. Pipe tall mounds of batter into the pan, about 1/2 cup each.
  7. Add 1 teaspoon water to the pan, and cover with a lid to steam. Cook on low heat, until pancakes have set and no longer look wet on top, about 8 minutes.
  8. Carefully flip pancakes and add another teaspoon water. Cover and finish cooking for 3 to 4 more minutes.
  9. Serve pancakes with whipped cream and blueberry sauce.
